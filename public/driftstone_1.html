01
Traditional rules

Five ancient stones drift across a 7×7 board. You can't pick them up or place them directly. Everything you do moves them indirectly — by placing directional Push Tiles that blow stones across the board and shift the field in one sweeping motion.

Form a pattern of three Driftstones and you win the game. First to three wins takes the match.

The catch: every tile you place stays on the board permanently. Over time the field fills with redirectors — a stone sent flying might ricochet through several tiles before it stops.
Turn 4 — Push Tiles directing Driftstones across the board.
02
How to win

At the end of your turn, if any three Driftstones form one of these patterns — anywhere on the board, any rotation — you win immediately.

LINE
Three in a continuous row or column
CORNER
Three corners of any 2×2 square
SLANT
A bent diagonal — any rotation
03
Push Tiles — the core action

Place one Push Tile on any empty square. Each tile has a direction: ↑ ↓ ← →. Placing triggers two effects in strict order:

1. Sunwise shift fires first
The priority stone for that direction shifts one step sunwise. A ↑ tile shifts the topmost stone right. If two stones are tied, both shift. The stone about to be blown is exempt from this shift.
2. Direct blow fires second
The tile fires along its row or column. The nearest Driftstone slides as far as it can — stopping before a wall, the Heartstone, or another stone.

If it couldn't move, it ricochets one step sunwise instead.

Max 2 tiles per direction per player. Each player has 8 tiles total. Tiles are permanent — stones sliding over a tile mid-move are immediately redirected, potentially chaining across the whole board.

05
The Heartstone

The square piece at the center. Blocks movement and tile placement during normal play.

Sunwise / Duskwise: The Heartstone facing determines the direction of all global shifts. Sunwise = clockwise. Duskwise = counter-clockwise — reversing every shift on the board. The winner chooses whether to flip it before the next game.
Late game: Once either player places all 8 tiles, the Heartstone becomes movable. It counts as one of three points in a winning pattern. Win with it in the pattern and you may place it anywhere on the board before the next game.
06
Between games

The winner moves the Heartstone one step in any direction before the board resets. If it lands on a Driftstone's starting square, that stone is displaced to the center. The winner also chooses whether to flip the flow direction.

Unstable Anchor: If the starting board already contains a valid pattern, all Driftstones shift one step before Turn 1 begins.

How to Play

DRIFTSTONE

Set the pattern. Turn the current.

01 — The Goal

Form a pattern of three.
Your opponent is trying to do the same.

Place Push Tiles to move the shared Driftstones across the board. The first player to arrange three Driftstones into a valid pattern wins the game. First to 3 wins takes the match.

02 — The Pieces

Five shared stones. One anchor. Eight tiles each.

Nobody owns the Driftstones — both players push the same five stones around the board. The Heartstone is a fixed obstacle no stone can enter.

03 — Push Tiles

Every tile you place does two things.

Place a directional Push Tile on any empty square. It fires two effects simultaneously:

Direct blow — the nearest stone in the arrow's line slides as far as it can go, stopping at a wall, the Heartstone, or another stone.

Sunwise shift — the topmost / rightmost / bottommost / leftmost stone (matching the arrow's direction) steps one square in the current's direction.

04 — The Current

The Heartstone sets the flow of the board.

The Heartstone faces either Sunwise (clockwise) or Duskwise (counter-clockwise). This determines which way the global shift fires on every single Push Tile played — by either player. Flip it between games to reshape the whole field.

05 — Tracks

Tiles stay on the board. They become traps.

Once placed, a Push Tile is permanent — until someone uses Pull the Push to rotate it. Any stone that slides over a tile mid-move is immediately redirected by it, firing a new blow and shift in that tile's direction. Players are laying track. Chain reactions are intentional.

06 — Special Actions

Each player has four tools. Each used once.

Pull the Push Rotate any tile on the board to a new direction. Fires immediately. Resets when you've placed all 8 tiles.
Binding Lock one Driftstone in place for two turns. Bound stones ignore all blows and shifts. Neither player can move them.
Seize the Pull Available from Turn 5. Take two full actions in a row — then your opponent takes two consecutive turns to compensate.
Move Heartstone Unlocks when either player exhausts all 8 tiles. Move the Heartstone anywhere as your action. It can form part of a winning pattern.
07 — Between Games

Winners reshape the board.

After winning a game, you move the Heartstone one step in any direction — including diagonally. You can also flip the current from Sunwise to Duskwise or back. If your new position would create an instant pattern, the Unstable Anchor fires: all stones shift one step before the game begins.

Win using the Heartstone as part of your pattern and you earn a bonus: place it anywhere on the board before the next game.
